Mahendra Singh Dhoni

MS Dhoni, born July 7, 1981, is a legendary Indian cricketer known as "Captain Cool" for his calm leadership, leading India to win the 2007 T20 World Cup, 2011 ODI World Cup, and 2013 Champions Trophy. A prolific wicket-keeper batsman from Ranchi, he became India's most successful captain, excelling with power-hitting and unorthodox keeping, also winning IPL titles with Chennai Super Kings. Mahendra Singh Dhoni, born July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, is a legendary Indian cricketer known as "Captain Cool" for his calm leadership, guiding India to historic wins in the 2007 T20 World Cup, 2011 ODI World Cup (hitting the winning six), and 2013 Champions Trophy. A dynamic wicketkeeper-batsman, he rose from humble beginnings, even working as a railway ticket examiner, to become one of cricket's best finishers. Dhoni captained India in most international matches, leading them to world No. 1 in Tests, and later achieved immense IPL success with Chennai Super Kings, retiring from international cricket in 2020.
